## What the client likes

Fiona’s preferred sites consistently use:

- calm, simple pages with generous white space;
- natural, warm imagery rather than overtly “medical” graphics;
- clear appointment calls to action;
- approachable, reassuring copy;
- high-quality, consistent clinician portraits;
- gentle colour palettes with restrained accents.

The sites she disliked are substantially denser. The clearest lesson is to avoid mega-navigation, exhaustive service lists above the fold, repeated sales claims and long blocks of text.

## Positioning and conversion strategy

The primary visitor is often anxious, uncertain or enquiring for someone they care about. The page therefore needs to answer four questions quickly:

1. Will I feel safe and understood here?
2. Do you help someone like me?
3. Who will I be speaking with?
4. What happens if I contact you?

Primary CTA: **Request an appointment**  
Secondary CTA: **Call the practice**

Trust signals used:

- established in 2005;
- postgraduate-qualified, AHPRA-registered clinical psychologists;
- real staff portraits;
- ACPA membership;
- evidence-based therapies including CBT, ACT, EMDR and Schema Therapy;
- clear, verified fee range and referral information;
- the real location and access information.

## Logo recommendation

The refreshed mark combines the two directions Fiona asked to explore:

- the calm, protective arch from concept 3;
- the existing upward dotted-arrow motif, simplified and redrawn;
- a modern Figtree wordmark;
- a muted eucalyptus, forest and clay palette in place of the bright green rounded square.

This preserves recognition while looking considered, contemporary and suitable for a specialist clinical practice.

## Content guardrails used

- No invented reviews, awards, waitlist claims or outcomes.
- No exact Medicare rebate amount, because rates can change.
- Fees use the current published range of $200–$265 for a 55-minute session.
- The contact form asks for contact details only and explicitly discourages sensitive health information.
- The generated consultation-room hero is decorative imagery and is not presented as Denton’s real premises.
- Crisis information remains prominent but discreet.
